01

BOOK COVER DESIGN

This high school media arts project involved designing a book cover in the thriller genre using Photoshop. I ensured all images were royalty-free for ethical sourcing. The process included selecting and editing photos, integrating typography to complement the mood, and refining elements like color grading and composition. Key Photoshop techniques, such as layer masks, blending modes, and custom textures, were used to bring the design concept to life and create a polished final product.

In addition to the technical aspects, I focused on building a narrative through visual elements that captured the mystery and intrigue of the thriller genre. The project challenged me to evaluate design choices, from font selection to image placement, ensuring they aligned with the tone. It also refined my workflow, enhancing my ability to manage layers and make precise adjustments. Ultimately, the experience boosted my confidence and strengthened my understanding of how design can engage and captivate an audience.

02

PRODUCT POSTER DESIGN

This project was completed as part of my Communications
Technology course, where the primary goal was to select a
product and design a visually compelling advertisement for
it using Photoshop.The objective was not only to effectively
present the product but also to demonstrate a thorough
understanding of Photoshop techniques. This included applying
concepts such as layout design, colour theory, and typography
to ensure the ad was both visually appealing. We were encouraged
to experiment with tools like layer effects, blending modes, and
image retouching to enhance the overall composition and create
a polished, professional-quality final piece. By focusing on both
technical execution and aesthetic appeal, I was able to create a
design that demonstrated skills in Photoshop but also my ability
to create engaging and impactful visual content.

03

PERSONAL POSTERS

This project was completed as part of GBDA 202, with the goal
of creating a digital self-portrait as a designer through the
medium of posters. I created four unique posters, each
highlighting a different aspect of my identity as a designer:
my skills, my favorite designer, my goals, and my design
principles. The overarching objective was to communicate
my story and style as a designer. I used Photoshop as my
primary tool, allowing me to experiment with layering,
textures, and effects to bring my vision to life. Through
this process, I was able to explore how different visual
elements, such as color palettes and composition, could
work together to create a cohesive and impactful narrative.

04

GBDA 101 DIPTYCH
POSTERS

This project was created as part of GBDA 101, focusing
on combining photography and design. The goal was
to capture original photographs and incorporate them
into two diptych posters. Each diptych was designed
to showcase a cohesive theme, blending imagery
and layout to convey a story or concept. We were
encouraged to select a specific theme for our posters,
and I chose to create a design with an eerie, thriller
inspired atmosphere.To achieve this, I carefully composed
my photographs to emphasize dramatic lighting, shadows,
and unique angles that added a sense of mystery and
tension. I experimented with textures, colour grading,
and typography to enhance the suspenseful feel.
