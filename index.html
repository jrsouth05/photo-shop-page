01

BOOK COVER DESIGN

This project was completed for a project in my high school media arts class. The objective was to pick a genre and using photoshop create a book cover. All photos used are royalty free, and the design reflects my chosen genre, which was thriller. The process involved selecting and editing images to align with the theme, adding typography that complements the mood, and experimenting with colour grading and composition to achieve a professional-looking final product. Through this project, I developed a stronger understanding of visual storytelling and learned how to effectively use Photoshop tools such as layer masks, blending modes, and custom textures.

03 ANIMAL CROSSING 3D DESIGN

This project was completed out of personal interest and was inspired by one of my favourite games, Animal Crossing on the
Nintendo Switch. For my Grade 12 design class, we were instructed to create a hero page for a favourite company, and I chose to
focus on Animal Crossing. This 3D vector design allowed me to explore new design skills, such as 3D modelling, while ensuring
my work aligned with an existing brand identity. Through this project, I gained valuable experience in balancing creativity with
brand consistency.

04

DIPTYCH POSTERS

This project was created as part of GBDA 101, focusing on combining photography and design. The goal was to capture original photographs and incorporate them into two diptych posters. Each diptych was designed to showcase a cohesive theme, blending imagery and layout to convey a story or concept. We were encouraged to select a specific theme for our posters, and I chose to create a design with an eerie, thriller-inspired atmosphere.
